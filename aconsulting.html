Hyper Automation

What is hyper automation? Well as Gartner defines, hyper-automation “deals with the application of advanced technologies, including artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML), to increasingly automate processes and augment humans. Hyper-automation extends across a range of tools that can be automated, but also refers to the sophistication of the automation (i.e., discover, analyse, design, automate, measure, monitor, reassess.)” Or to put it simply a mixture of automation technology that enchances or augments human capability. Due to hyper automation's ability to augment human prodction it comes with great benefits:

  • It can reduces human effort by doing a lot of work in lesser amount of time
  • Thus can reduce the risk of human errors as machines are for precision
  • Automation can also improve the quality of jobs by eliminating tedious, tiring, and dangerous for humans processes
  • This then leads to better productivity and much higher output
But what are the practical downsides? There isn't a lot to say on this due to how new the technology is. But one thing is for certain hyper automation isn't cheap to implement. However the potential returns makes this worthwhile.

Distributed Cloud

Distributed Cloud is having computational, storage, and networking ability in a smaller cloud service located outside the centralized cloud. This in turn offers a more flexible and safer option for a company, as they aren't reliant on a singular massive cloud nor do they suffer the costs of on site servers. The decentralisation of the cloud comes with measurable benefits too:

  • The aforementioned resilience too failure is one of its big draw ins
  • It's networking scalability is a big advantage. Instead of buying two massive clouds and using some of each, you can instead buy a group for smaller clouds you can use to its full potential so everything is pushed for potential
  • Distributed clouds are also secure. Since they are spread apart, should a data breach occured then the damage is minimalised.
